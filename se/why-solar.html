why Electric vehicles

why Electric vehicles

Globally, automotive industry is passing through a paradigm of shift. The 20th century has been era of Internal Combustion Engines (ICE) Primarily on account of accessibility – ease of use and affordability-low-cost of fossil fuels. The shift to electric mobility has become necessary due to fast depletion of fossil fuels, increase in energy costs, impact of transportation on environment and concerns over climate change.

As per International Energy Agency (IEA) report of 2009, globally the fossil fuel based transportation is the second largest source of CO2 emissions. From 2006 to 2030, the global energy consumption is estimated to increase by 53% and about three quarter of this projected will be due to the oil demand in transportation.

Electric vehicle (EV) of all types lie at the heart of future sustainable transport system due to the advantages they offer: eco friendliness from systemic standpoint; cheaper fuel cost; lower maintenance expenses. The deployment of EVs across all models is also in line with the 2030 United Nations Sustainability agenda.

Every day, Nearly 50,000 new motors vehicles (Two, three and four wheelers) are registered in India. With a 10% increase in registration annually. As on May - 2017. Bangalore alone witnessed the registration of 69,31,873 vehicles of different categories out of which the two wheelers segment had a major share of 48,00,298 followed by the private cars whose share stood at 13,40,424. Bengaluru is also known as a city which registers maximum numbers of two wheelers in the country.

According to the 2016 World health origination study, India is home to 10 of the World’s most polluted cities. India imported more than 80% of its oil at a cost of Rs 4.2 lakh crore.
