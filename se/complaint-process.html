FAQ'S

What is an inverter?

The inverter is the device that monitors and converts the DC electricity produced by the solar panels to AC. The inverter can be single phase or three phase depending on the application.

How much roof space is required ?

On a flat roof we need around 8msq or 70 sqft to install 1kw system. However, the space varies based on the design of the building like facing direction, available space etc.

Do the panels need to face a certain direction and at a specific angle?

Yes, the direction depends on the Latitude. In Bangalore the direction of the panels will be south and the inclination angle would be 13 degrees (approx.)

Do My meters need to be changed?

Yes, we need to install a bi-directional meter. This meter is required to monitor and calculate how much electricity is consumed and exported by the household. And your old meter is used to measure solar electricity generation.

Do I need to perform any maintenance on the panels?

It is always advisable to clean the panels by moping with wet cloth once in 15 days to have maximum efficiency.

Can I upgrade the system at a later date?

Yes, I we have installed a bigger inverter, upgradation is possible.

Is the energy produced affected if the panels get dirty?

Efficiency of panels will be great if it is maintained clean. Panels not cleaned for months may reduce its efficiency by 10%.

Is there any warranty provided with Solar Panel System?

Yes, we provide one the best warranties in the market with 5 years manufacturer warranty on Inverter, 10 years warranty on panels and 2 years warranty on workmanship and batteries.

Does solar systems work effectively in winter?

Solar Photovoltaic system needs solar radiation to generate electricity. PV systems will work even when the weather is cloudy. However, the efficiency will be high in direct sunlight.

In event of a supply failure or blackout does the unit take over supply of the house?

No. For safety reasons, when the normal supply of electricity is interrupted the solar system will shut down. However, depending on the customer requirement, we can provide hybrid systems which run when there is power failure.

How long will it take for my installation to take place after making my booking?

It takes around 6 – 8 weeks to install the system. As a process we need to get permission from Government bodies like BESCOM for installation. After receiving their permission it will take 5 – 6 working days to install and integrate with grid.

How effective is a solar system in terms of value for money?

Gross metering systems takes 6years and net metering system takes around 7 years to break even. Then after power consumption will be free and government provides feed in per unit supplied back to grid for 25 years from the date of the agreement.

When I get solar power do I have to enter a new contract with my electricity retailer for the electricity I buy?

Yes, as a process of applying permission for installing solar systems, new contract will be entered.

How much electricity will I generate?

A 3kw system can generate up to 15.5 – 16 units of electricity in sunny weather and 4.5 – 6 units of electricity in cloudy weather. On an average 1kw system can produce 4.2 units electricity per day.

Will I generate enough to sell power back to the grid?

An average home consumption is around 5 units a day. Considering that 3kw system is installed which generates 12.5 units a day in average allows you to supply 7.5 units a day back to the grid and earn returns from government.

What is the difference between net metering and gross metering?

Gross metering measures all the power your solar panels produce and all the power that you use in the house. This type of metering is used where there is a gross feed in tariff. Under gross metering, you are paid the gross feed in tariff for all the power you produce and you must pay the usage tariff for all the power that you consume. Net metering measures only the difference between what your solar power systems are producing and what you are consuming. Under net metering, you are paid the net feed in tariff for the excess power you produce and you must pay the usage tariff for any power that you import.

Will I need a new meter and will I have to pay for it?

Yes, while integrating solar PV systems we install two meters one for measuring solar generation and other for export and import (bi-directional).
